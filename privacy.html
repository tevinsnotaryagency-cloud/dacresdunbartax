Privacy Policy

How we collect, use, and protect your information

Effective Date: January 1, 2025  |  Dacres & Dunbar Tax Service

1. Information We Collect

When you interact with Dacres & Dunbar Tax Service — whether through our website, forms, phone, or email — we may collect the following information:

  • Full name
  • Phone number (mobile and/or landline)
  • Email address
  • Form submission details and messages
  • Financial and tax-related information you provide for service delivery
  • Website usage data (via cookies and analytics tools)

2. How We Use Your Information

  • Scheduling and confirming appointments
  • Delivering tax preparation and advisory services
  • Communicating about your account or service requests
  • Sending SMS messages (if you have opted in) including appointment reminders, service updates, and support communications
  • Responding to inquiries and providing customer support
  • Improving our website and services

3. SMS Consent & Messaging

If you provide your phone number and check the SMS consent box on our contact form, you agree to receive text messages from Dacres & Dunbar Tax Service related to appointments, services, and support.

Reply STOP to opt out at any time. Reply HELP for assistance. Message and data rates may apply. Message frequency may vary.

SMS consent is never a condition of receiving our services.

4. How We Share Your Information

We do not share or sell your personal information — including mobile numbers — to third parties for marketing purposes. We may share data with service providers who assist us only as necessary to provide the services described in this policy.

We may disclose information if required by law, such as in response to a valid legal request or to comply with IRS regulations.

5. Cookies & Analytics

Our website may use cookies and similar tracking technologies to enhance your browsing experience and gather anonymized usage data. We may use third-party analytics tools (such as Google Analytics) that collect anonymized information about website traffic. You may disable cookies in your browser settings; however, some features of the site may not function properly.

6. Data Security

We implement reasonable administrative, technical, and physical safeguards to protect your data against unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, or destruction. However, no method of transmission over the internet is 100% secure.

7. Data Retention

We retain client information for as long as necessary to provide our services and comply with applicable tax and legal regulations. You may request deletion of your information (where legally permissible) by contacting us directly.

8. Your Rights

You have the right to access, correct, or request deletion of your personal information (subject to applicable law). To exercise these rights, please contact us at the information below.
